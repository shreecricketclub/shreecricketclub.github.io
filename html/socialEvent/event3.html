Project Bakulabai

The reality of human life is that we are unique individuals who can only function by pooling our efforts as a society.

Importance of our daily efforts to make this world a better place, no matter how insignificant those efforts may seem to us. Because in the end, none of us has the power to change things alone… but we do have the power to help change things. We can add our drop. Call it a drop in the ocean or a drop in the bucket. Whatever. It’s an important drop.

Inspired by the same belief and urge to go beyond cricket, and contribute to the society Shree Cricket Club played a vital role in helping a poor family in Ganuwadi near Baramati.

Bakulabai, an old aged woman, mother of two daughters and a grandson. One of her daughters is mentally ill and the other has a spin injury due to which she cannot get out of the bed. Bakulabai was the sole breadwinner of the family before her grandson, who is hardly 15 years of age, had to give up studies and support the family.

Nothing was going well for Bakulabai, as this was not enough she got struck with one more tragedy. Her house was burnt down to ashes by fire. The cause and origin of fire was unknown. This added to her wounds, she had to sell all her goats to rebuild the house (with that money she could only afford a roof and 4 pillars). As the goats were the only source of her income she was all broke.

After coming to know about the tragic story of Bakulabai, Shree Cricket Club offered a helping hand to her. All the members of Shree Cricket Club, their friends and colleagues generously offered help in whatever way they can.

On the auspicious day of 15th August, Shree Cricket Club headed to Ganuwadi with all the supplies they had gathered. They offered a bicycle to Bakulabai's grandson, clothes, some grocery and goats.

We gain both as individuals and as a society if we focus our lives on adding our special drop to that pool of water. We elevate our own existence and help to elevate the existence of our fellow human beings. And who knows? Our drop may turn out to be a few drops — or a whole cupful of water.

After keeping in regular touch , we are glad to share that on our support visit in Feb 2013 , we could see the new hutment ready for living and the resources provided being very useful. And on "Gudipadwa" the family entered the home ! So, a small but timely help could lift the spirits of so many lives .......
